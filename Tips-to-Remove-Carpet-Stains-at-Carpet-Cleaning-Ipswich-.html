For those pet lovers out there, one important thing you should be ready to cope with is cleaning the mess your pet left behind. This is often irritating to say the least. Below are a few strategies for tackling the task to eliminate dog or cat stains and any related odour from the carpet.

To begin with, clean up the initial mess. After that, focus on the stain and when it has already dried up, moisten it first before you apply a carpet stain removal product. Though there are lots of specially formulated products available for purchase, you could make your own pet stains removal solution naturally. Simply mix 1/4 cup of white vinegar with two pints of warm water and spray directly on the stain. Put adequate solution to soak the spot, then carry on with the blot technique until the stain is visibly gone.


With regards to blood stain removal, you have to treat the stain as quickly as possible. You need to use only cold water and not hot or tepid water when dealing with blood stains on carpeting. The reason is , heat literally cooks the blood that causes the stain to permanently set right into the carpeting fibres.

Ink Spot


In the listing of hard to remove carpet stains, ink stains will leave the carpet in ruins if not treated properly. There are some good ways to remove ink carpet stains. One simple technique is to dampen a white cloth with isopropyl alcohol and then proceed by dabbing the towel over the stained carpet. Let it sit for a little bit and then vacuum it up.

Coffee


Coffee stains could leave an unsightly yellowish/brown tone on the carpeting which could easily be mistaken as an overlooked pet mess. Just like most stains, your initial job should be to try and get rid of as much liquid out of your carpet using a clean paper towel. Again, you can try with a blend of vinegar and water by spraying it on the stain. If doesn't work, put a non-bleach detergent to the mixture. If the stain continues to be visible, rinse and repeat the process right up until it is really removed. Stain removal is not hard with certain kinds of carpets such as polypropylene.

Always try out a stain removal product on an inconspicuous area before utilizing on the stained spot as it will allow you to ensure that your cleaning product is carpet-friendly.
