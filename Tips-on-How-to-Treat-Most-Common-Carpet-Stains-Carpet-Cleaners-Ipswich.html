Carpet stains and spills are likely to happen to every person at some point or another and as such, it is important to act quickly to get the blemish out of the carpet. Since staining does occur out of your control, we'll focus on the different cleaning solutions that will help remove the stains without damaging the carpet fibres. Your carpet is susceptible to different types of stains. Because of this, different sets of techniques are in place to get rid of different stains. Below are a few of the common carpet stains and guidelines on how to get rid of them.

Pets Stains on Carpets


For those pet lovers out there, one important thing you need to be ready to deal with is clearing up the mess your furry friend left behind. Even if your pet is house trained, you do not feel surprised when you notice him urinating inside your home. The great thing is there are plenty of methods you may pursue in getting rid of pet stains and then any related smell.

The initial step certainly is the nastiest - cleaning the mess itself. After that, youâ€™ll need to clean up the stain; in the event the spot has already dried out, it will need to be dampened before using a pet stain removing solution. For pet stains, there are plenty of cleaning products on the market today however, you could make your own solution. Create a 50-50 mix of white wine vinegar and water and spirt it directly on the spot. Permit the solution to soak for several minutes, after which use the blot technique until the stain is gone.


When dealing with blood stains, the faster you function, the more effective the stain removal method will be. Always use cold water instead of warm water when treating blood spots on the carpeting. This is because heat pretty much cooks the blood that can cause the stain to permanently set right into the carpeting fibres.

Coffee Stains


Coffee spots will leave a carpet looking soiled and dark brown if not treated properly. Your initial task is always to blot the area using some tissue paper. After that, create a mix of vinegar, water, and non-bleach soaps and spray it on the stain. Repeat the process mentioned above till the spot is no more visible.

Ink Stains


Ink stains can wreck havoc on the fibres of your carpets and could sometimes ruin carpet if they are not cleaned thoroughly. In several ways, you could eliminate the ink stains from your carpeting but the majority of them involve a lot of elbow work. Many times, the rubbing alcohol is sufficient to remove the ink stain. Simply apply it over the stain by using a clean cloth. Do not scrub or rub because this can spread the ink.
